MMLU-Pro Model Family Analysis

Comparative Evaluation of Reasoning Scaffolding across Claude 4.5 Family

Accuracy Impact

Key Finding

Scaffolding hurts capable models. While Haiku (42%) sees neutral impact, Sonnet (-30%) and Opus (-7%) perform significantly worse when forced to follow explicit reasoning steps compared to their native baseline performance.

Cost Efficiency

Unexpected Efficiency

For Sonnet and Opus, scaffolding actually reduced costs. The structured prompt likely caused the models to be more concise or conclude earlier, whereas the baseline "natural" reasoning was more verbose.
